CS6491: Topics in Optimization and its Applications in Computer Science

Lecture Info

Prof. Chee Wei Tan, Every Tuesday 9:00-11:50 am, Room LI-1406.

Course Description

The goal of this course is to expose students to modern and fundamental developments of optimization theory, algorithms and applications in computer science. The course covers the conceptual and algorithmic sides of convex optimization as well as dynamic programming, whose rich expressive power makes it suitable to address a wide spectrum of important problems arising in mathematics and computer science.
We will cover cone programming, which includes linear programming, quadratic programming and semidefinite programming, and also geometric programming and dynamic programming. On the algorithmic side, the course covers efficient methods including optimization decomposition, convex relaxation and proximal algorithms to address large-scale problems and non-convex problems. Emphasis will also be placed on the software aspect of convex optimization and dynamic programming. A variety of applications and research problems in computer science will be selectively drawn from combinatorial graph problems, Internet and wireless networks, online social networks, machine learning, statistical inference and artificial intelligence.
